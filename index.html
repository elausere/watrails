The Enchantments

  • Location: Okanogan-Wenatchee National Forest
  • Length: 18 miles, one way
  • Elevation: 4500 ft. gain | 7800 ft. highpoint

Hard

The Enchantments is the epitome of high alpine exploration in Washington. The grueling 18-mile trail can be done in multiple days by obtaining a much sought-after permit. For a real challenge, the entire trail can also be done in one long day of hiking.The sweat equity is well worth the views, though. The trail passes by several craggy peaks and interlinking alpine lakes. A good trail for seasoned hikers seeking more.

Skyline Trail Loop

  • Location: Mount Rainer National Park
  • Length: 5.5 miles roundtrip
  • Elevation: 1450 ft. gain | 6800 ft. highpoint

Moderate

The main hiking trail in the Paradise hiking area of Mount Rainier National Park, the Skyline Trail is one of the most popular trails to explore the scenery around Washington's most iconic mountain. Despite its crowds in the peak summer season, the Skyline Trail offers plenty of scenery to share. The trail itself is wider than average trails, and it includes access to longer trails in the area.

Lake 22

  • Location: Mt. Baker Snoqualmie National Forest
  • Length: 5.4 miles roundtrip
  • Elevation: 1350 ft. gain | 2400 ft. highpoint

Moderate

Lake 22 Trail is a 5.7 mile heavily trafficked loop trail located near Granite Falls, Washington that features a waterfall and is rated as moderate. Dogs are also able to use this trail but must be kept on leash.The hike to the lake combines the best of mountain rainforests, old-growth, wetlands, and mountain views, yet it is readily accessible. A must go for anyone in the area or those willing to make the drive.

Wallace Falls

  • Location: Wallace Falls State Park
  • Length: 5.6 miles roundtrip
  • Elevation: 1300 ft. gain | 1500 ft. highpoint

Moderate

The Wallace Falls Trail is one of the most popular trails in the state of Washington. It's not just the easy accessibility and moderate grade that draws crowds to this state park, but also the incredible scenery found on every stretch of this trip.The trail encompasses nine different waterfalls on the Wallace River and each dazzling display of cascading water is worth the trip itself.

Colchuck Lake

  • Location: Alpine Lakes Wilderness Leavenworth
  • Length: 8.0 miles roundtrip
  • Elevation: 2280 ft. gain | 5580 ft. highpoint

Hard

Next to Stuart Lake, this has to be one of the most glorious day trips in the area. This hike is challenging in that it is mostly up, and up, and up. However, the views at the lake are well worth the effort. Many great spots for swimming and snoozing. The trail is primarily used for hiking, camping, and backpacking and is best used from May until October. Also, theres a good chance you'll see several adorable chipmunks

Franklin Falls

  • Location: Mt. Baker Snoqualmie National Forest
  • Length: 2.0 Miles Roundtrip
  • Elevation: 400 ft. gain | 2600 ft. highpoint

Easy

An easy 2 mile roundtrip hike that leads to beautiful Franklin Falls and is easy for even young hikers to travel. The trail goes through the forest and along a gorge as you trek to the falls. There are a few places in the gorge which has a few really small waterfalls in it, but non of these falls compare to that of Franklin Falls. Franklin Falls leads into a river that is shallow enough to walk across.

Cascade Pass

  • Location: North Cascades National Park
  • Length: 7 miles roundtrip
  • Elevation: 1800 ft. gain | 5392 ft. highpoint

Moderate

The Cascade Pass Trail is one of the most accessible trails in the relatively remote North Cascades National Park. The trail provides some of the easiest terrain to take you deep into this wondrous mountain environment. The multiple views of craggy Cascade peaks and the glaciated valleys that define them, the Cascade Pass Trail is worth the sometimes-crowded conditions.

Ape Caves

  • Location: Mt. Saint Helens Volcanic Monument
  • Length: 2.8 miles roundtrip
  • Elevation: 350 ft. gain | 2450 ft. highpoint

Easy

Within Mount Saint Helens National Volcanic Monument, the Ape Cave Trail plunges visitors into a subterranean world. Extending for more than two miles underground, the trail provides access to one of the longest lava tubes in the nation where anyone can explore this new world beneath their feet. For more adventurous explorers, the upper cave offers more moderate difficulty.

Sol Duc Falls

  • Location: Olympic National Park
  • Length: 1.6 Miles Roundtrip
  • Elevation: 200 ft gain | 1951 ft. highpoint

Easy

Sol Duc Falls Nature Trail is a 1.6 mile heavily trafficked out and back trail located near Joyce, Washington that features a lake and is good for all skill levels. The route is family friendly and pleasant, wandering beneath a dense forest canopy and among every shade of green. Cross a small stream on a bridge, pausing to enjoy the water tumbling over moss-covered rocks. Each step of the way is a sight to see.
